IELTS Listening Practice Test

The IELTS Listening examination is expected to last around 30 minutes, during which you will be given an additional 10 minutes to transfer your answers onto the answer sheet.


It is advisable that you attentively listen the instructions provided for each section of the test. You are expected to respond to all 40 questions, with each question carrying one mark. It is crucial to listen attentively to the instructions provided for each segment of the paper.


In each segment of the test, there will be time for you to look through the questions and time for you to check your answers.Upon finishing all four components of the Listening test, a period of ten minutes will be provided for you to transcribe your answers onto a distinct answer sheet.


Test takers are advised not to open the question paper until explicitly instructed to do so. Please ensure that your name and candidate number are promptly written in the designated spaces at the top of this page.

Useful tips for the IELTS listening examination

  • Before the recording begins for any section, do read all the questions carefully. This will help follow the recordings and identify the answers easily.
  • At times, there will be a list of options to make a pick from. It is not necessary that the answers will be in the order you hear them, they may could also be listed alphabetically.
  • Do note the word limits for each section. If an instruction says, ‘Write no more than two words’, stick to it.
  • Be attentive and look out for the key words or synonyms
  • while you are listening, write your answers on the question paper
  • while listening to a recording, you can keep crossing out the options that do not fit. It makes it easier to find the right option.
  • If you face trouble in answering any question, leave it and move on.
